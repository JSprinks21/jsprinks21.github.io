Speed Cubing

What is Speed Cubing?

The Rubik's Cube, first invented by Erno Rubik, is quite possibly the best-selling puzzle game in the world. With over 43 quintillion possible arrangements, many consider the cube to be virtually unsolvable. In reality, it is fairly easy to learn how to solve. In fact, it has been proven that no matter how it is scrambled, the 3x3 Rubik's Cube can be solved in 20 moves or less. Although it would be quite difficult for a human to determine the optimal solution to a scrambled cube, it can easily be solved in well under 100 moves, and there is a thriving community of people, called speed cubers, who compete to solve the Rubik's Cube and other similar puzzles as fast as possible. Cubing Methods

  • Fridrich/CFOP
    1. Cross: Solve a cross on the bottom of the cube.
    2. F2L (First Two Layers): Solve the first two layers by pairing corner and edge pieces and inserting them into the bottom two layers.
    3. OLL (Orient Last Layer): Use one of 57 algorithms to orient the last layer.
    4. PLL (Permute Last Layer): Use one of 21 algorithms to permute the last layer.
  • Roux
    1. First Block: Solve a 1x2x3 block.
    2. Second Block: Solve a second 1x2x3 block directly opposite the first.
    3. CMLL/COLL: Orient and permute the last layer corners.
    4. EO (Edge Orientation): Orient the last six edges.
    5. LSE (Last Six Edges): Permute the last six edges.
  • ZZ
    1. EOLine: Simultaneously orient all edges, and solve a line on the bottom of the cube from front to back.
    2. F2L (First Two Layers: Solve the first two layers.
    3. LL (Last Layer): The last layer will already be oriented, so it just needs to be permuted.
  • Petrus
    1. Build a 2x2x2 block.
    2. Expand the block to a 2x2x3.
    3. Orient the edges.
    4. Finish first two layers.
    5. Permute last layer corners.
    6. Orient last layer corners.
    7. Permute last layer edges.
